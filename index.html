Given a graph G, \(T=\{v|d_G(v)\equiv1\ mod\ 2\}\), a subgraph P is called a parity subgraph if \[ d_P(v)\equiv\left\{ \begin{aligned} &0\ mod\ 2,\ v\not\in T\\ &1\ mod\ 2,\ v\in T \end{aligned} \right.\qquad i.e.\ d_P(v)\equiv d_G(v) \] \(G-E(P)\) is an even subgraph ( i.e. every vertex of G-E(P) has even degree)
Problem: To find a parity subgraph with the minimum number of edges.
